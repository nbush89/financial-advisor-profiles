Overview

Betterment is a leader among Robo-Advisors. They offer tools to help you manage your money through cash management, guided investing, and retirement planning. They offer a free version with no account minimum as well as a premium version that includes unlimited access to financial planners. Both plans charge rates as a percentage of assets under management annually. Betterment is ideal for hands-off investors and those with low balances.

Pros

  • No account minimum to have a digital account, premium requires $100,000.
  • Inexpensive management fees
  • Financial planning packages
  • Tools for goal-based saving
  • Offers retirment planning - can sync existing accounts

Cons

  • No direct indexing
  • Suggests putting funds in a safety net account, contary to conventional advice.
  • If you want to leave the company, transferring funds out requires a good deal of mailed in paperwork.

Retirement Planning

With Betterment you have access to retirement planning tools without funding an account by syncing existing accounts. Once you transfer retirement assets Betterment will charge a 0.25% management fee.

These tools offer suggestions on how you can save on each of your accounts. Betterments Tax Suite is estimated to save you 38% more over 30 years, compared to a typical retirement investor.
